User has read Chapter 2. Core material: architectural thinking, with emphasis on technical breadth vs. depth.

## Key concepts absorbed

**The Knowledge Pyramid** — three tiers:
1. Stuff you know (expertise / technical depth)
2. Stuff you know you don't know (awareness / technical breadth)
3. Stuff you don't know you don't know (blind spots)

**The key insight:** Developers optimize the top tier (depth). Architects optimize the middle tier (breadth). The transition from developer to architect requires a deliberate shift in *what* you invest in.

**The Transition Trap:** Developers on the path to architect keep investing in depth (learning another framework deeply) instead of breadth (gaining awareness of many options). Feels less productive but is the actual career-advancing move.

**Confirmation bias by familiarity:** Deep expertise creates gravitational pull toward known tools, even when they are a poor fit for the actual constraints.

- Can probe whether the user applies breadth-thinking instinctively: when evaluating technologies or design options, do they consider alternatives they haven't used?
